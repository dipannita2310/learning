Observation¶

-> From the model summary we can see that the parameters TAX and B is not significant at 5% significance level. Because p-values of the parameters (for TAX p-value is 0.888 and for B p-value is 0.063) are higher than 0.05 and confidence intervals for their co-efficient includes zeros .

-> On the other hand rest of the parameters are significant because their p-values are lower than 0.05.

Hence , we can conclude price of houses in Boston does not depends on TAX rate and B .

Let's try our inference and drop the column TAX 1st and the model again .

Note : If we can see the result we can see heteoscedasticity exixts between constant and parameter NOX . But constant term is not an actual parameter , this is y intercept . Hence I ignored heteoscedasticity .
